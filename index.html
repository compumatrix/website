Simple Agreement for Future Tokens

The SAFT aims to replace direct token pre-sales, where investors fund the creation of a network. The SAFT is an instrument that enables a seller (the network creators) to sell rights to receive tokens in the future to a purchaser (investor).

About

Since 2013, hundreds of token networks have used token sales to raise start-up funding all over the world. For five years, network creators and prospective investors suffered significant legal inclarity and uncertainty. Were such fundraisers selling unregistered securities? Would network creators and investors run afoul of securities laws, in the U.S. and other jurisdictions? Was this inclusive new form of crowdfunding simply misleading staunch supporters about risks and legality? During this period, network creators faced existential challenges that few could navigate. Many chose to abandon their countries and move their businesses to more certain jurisdictions, and in some cases banned U.S. and other investors from participating altogether.

Multiple independent network creators, investors, and lawyers arrived at the same conclusion: the industry needed a standard, compliant agreement to finance token networks. A few started using instruments derived from the popular SAFE, but these varied significantly and were not publicly analyzed. Protocol Labs, Cooley, AngelList, and CoinList collaborated extensively to create a standard framework for compliant token sales. Thus the SAFT Project began.

The SAFT Project is an extensive effort to formalize a framework for compliant token sales, across many jurisdictions. Today, the majority of the work has focused on U.S. regulations and finding a compliant path for fundraising token sales (usually pre-sales before the tokens are useful). We have a lengthy analysis of the current industry status-quo, and how the SAFT improves the ecosystem with token sales that fit the relevant laws.
